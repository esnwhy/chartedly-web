We spent four weeks testing six of the most popular options you'll find at any Matsumoto Kiyoshi or Welcia to help you pick the right one.

## How We Tested

We applied each sunscreen daily over four weeks during March and April in Tokyo. Each product was evaluated on UV protection specs, ease of application, white cast (tested on light, medium, and dark skin tones), staying power through a typical commute, and how well it sat under makeup. We also factored in price-per-gram to determine overall value.

## Where to Buy

All of these sunscreens are available at major Japanese drugstores like Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Sundrug, and Welcia — usually for the prices listed above. If you prefer shopping online or want to stock up before your trip, [Amazon Japan](https://www.amazon.co.jp) ships most of these domestically with next-day delivery. Some are also available on Amazon US and global beauty retailers, though prices tend to be marked up.
