# Chartedly Category Recommendation Report

**Prepared by:** ATLAS (Senior Strategy Consultant)
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Audience:** Foreign residents in Japan (3-4M), Tourists (~40M/year), Japanese users (ages 10-40)

---

## Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive research across tourist shopping guides, expat communities, Rakuten Global Express shipping data, Amazon Japan bestsellers, affiliate program structures, and competitor analysis (mybest.com), this report identifies the optimal product categories for Chartedly.

The strongest categories sit at the intersection of three factors:
1. **High foreigner demand** -- products foreigners actively search for and buy
2. **Comparison-friendly** -- categories where side-by-side comparison adds real value
3. **Monetizable** -- categories with viable affiliate programs and decent commission rates

## EXCLUDED Categories

These categories should NOT be prioritized for Chartedly.

| Category | Reason for Exclusion |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Real Estate / Property** | Not a product comparison category. Complex, localized, and better served by specialized platforms (Suumo, GaijinPot Apartments). |
| **Cars / Vehicles** | Too complex for a product comparison site. Requires dealer networks, test drives, financing. Not an online purchase. |
| **Insurance (Health/Life)** | Heavily regulated. Requires licensed advisors. Not suitable for simple product comparison. |
| **Financial Products (Loans, Mortgages)** | Regulated industry. Requires special licenses in Japan. |
| **Fresh / Perishable Food** | Cannot be shipped or compared meaningfully. Not an e-commerce play. |
| **Luxury Watches / Jewelry** | Counterfeiting concerns, authentication requirements, and very niche audience. Not a volume play. |
| **Musical Instruments** | Very niche. Low search volume from foreigners. Requires physical testing. |
| **Furniture / Large Appliances** | Shipping logistics make this impractical for tourists. Residents have IKEA/Nitori. Low affiliate value. |
| **Pet Products** | Niche within an already niche audience (foreigners with pets in Japan). Too small to prioritize early. |
| **DIY / Tools (non-kitchen)** | Low foreigner demand. Japanese hardware stores serve residents well. |
| **Tobacco / Vaping** | Regulatory restrictions on advertising and affiliate marketing. |
| **Generic Clothing (Fast Fashion)** | Foreigners don't come to Japan for H&M or GU comparisons. Only Japanese-specific brands have value. |

## Recommended Launch Strategy

### Phase 1: Launch (Month 1-2) -- 5 Core Categories
Focus on the highest-impact, highest-search-volume categories that serve BOTH tourists and residents:

1. **Sunscreen** -- Seasonal urgency, massive search volume, high affiliate rates
2. **Skincare** -- Evergreen demand, cult following, strong affiliate programs
3. **SIM Cards / eSIM / Pocket WiFi** -- 100% purchase intent, every tourist needs this
4. **OTC Medicine** -- Language barrier creates genuine need for English comparisons
5. **Kitchen Knives** -- High AOV, strong affiliate revenue, iconic Japan product

### Phase 2: Growth (Month 3-6) -- Add 5 Categories
6. **Cosmetics & Makeup**
7. **Supplements & Vitamins**
8. **Japanese Snacks** (traffic driver / SEO play)
9. **Rice Cookers & Kitchen Appliances**
10. **Anime & Manga Merch**

### Phase 3: Expansion (Month 6-12) -- Add Resident-Focused Categories
11. **Low-cost SIM / Mobile Plans** (high recurring commission)
12. **Credit Cards for Foreigners** (very high CPA)
13. **Hair Care**
14. **Stationery**
15. **Tea & Matcha**

## Competitive Landscape Note

**mybest.com** (30M monthly users in Japan) covers all of these categories in Japanese and is expanding to English. Chartedly's competitive advantage must be:

1. **English-first content** targeting foreigners specifically (not translated Japanese content)
2. **Foreigner-specific context** (voltage compatibility, allergy ingredients in English, where to buy tax-free, shipping overseas)
3. **Decision-support for Japan-specific confusion** (e.g., which OTC medicine label means what, which SIM works with my phone)
4. **Tourist-optimized content** (what to buy before your trip, in-store vs. online, duty-free tips)
