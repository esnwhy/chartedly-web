## 4. TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis

### 4.1 Total Addressable Market (TAM)

**Definition:** All affiliate revenue generated from product comparison and recommendation content in Japan.

- Japan affiliate marketing market (FY2024): **JPY 438.2 billion** (~USD 2.9 billion at 150 JPY/USD)
- Not all affiliate revenue comes from comparison sites; estimated comparison/recommendation share: ~30%
- **TAM = JPY 131.5 billion** (~USD 877 million)

Source: Yano Research FY2024 projection

### 4.2 Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

**Definition:** Affiliate revenue from product comparison content that serves Chartedly's audience segments (English-speaking residents, bilingual Japanese users ages 10--40, tourists researching purchases).

Addressable audience:
- Foreign residents actively shopping online: ~2.5 million (est. 60% of 4.13M)
- Tourists researching Japan products: ~10 million (est. 25% of 42.7M engage in pre-trip product research)
- Bilingual/English-curious Japanese ages 10--40: ~3 million (est. ~8% of 38M)
- **Total addressable users: ~15.5 million**

As a share of Japan's online shopping population (~85 million):
- Addressable share: ~18%
- **SAM = JPY 131.5B x 18% = JPY 23.7 billion** (~USD 158 million)

### 4.3 Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) -- Year 1-3

**Definition:** Revenue Chartedly can realistically capture given its bootstrapped, solo-founder constraints.

| Year | Monthly Unique Visitors | Affiliate CTR | Avg. Order Value (JPY) | Avg. Commission Rate | Est. Monthly Revenue (JPY) | Est. Annual Revenue (JPY) |
|------|------------------------|---------------|------------------------|---------------------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Year 1 | 50,000 | 3.0% | 5,000 | 4.0% | 300,000 | 3,600,000 |
| Year 2 | 150,000 | 3.5% | 5,500 | 4.5% | 1,299,375 | 15,592,500 |
| Year 3 | 400,000 | 4.0% | 6,000 | 5.0% | 4,800,000 | 57,600,000 |

**Calculation logic:**
- Monthly Revenue = Monthly Visitors x CTR x Avg. Order Value x Commission Rate
- Year 1: 50,000 x 0.03 x 5,000 x 0.04 = JPY 300,000/month
- Year 2: 150,000 x 0.035 x 5,500 x 0.045 = JPY ~1,299,375/month
- Year 3: 400,000 x 0.04 x 6,000 x 0.05 = JPY 4,800,000/month

**Conservative adjustment (50% of above):** Not all clicks convert to purchases. Applying an estimated 50% purchase conversion rate after click:

| Year | Adjusted Annual Revenue (JPY) | Adjusted Annual Revenue (USD) |
|------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Year 1 | 1,800,000 | ~12,000 |
| Year 2 | 7,796,250 | ~52,000 |

## 6. Consumer Behaviour Insights

### Product Discovery Pain Points for Foreigners in Japan

Based on expat community discussions and industry sources:

1. **Language barrier:** Most product reviews and comparison sites (Kakaku, mybest, @cosme) are Japanese-only, making informed purchasing extremely difficult for non-Japanese speakers
2. **Product format differences:** Foreigners often cannot find familiar products (deodorant, specific medications, clothing sizes) and need guidance on Japanese alternatives (sources: GaijinPot, Japan Today)
3. **Counterfeit concerns:** Online shoppers express frustration with counterfeit goods on marketplaces, seeking trusted recommendation sources (source: Accio/Reddit analysis)
4. **Overwhelming choice:** Japan's retail density (convenience stores, drugstores, Don Quijote) creates paradox of choice -- too many options with no English-language curation
5. **Import vs. local decision:** Foreigners frequently debate importing home-country products vs. finding Japanese equivalents, needing a trusted guide (source: backpackingman.com, expat forums)

### Japanese Consumer Preferences

- Consumers "heavily rely on recommendations (e.g., review and comparison sites) and influencer marketing when making purchasing decisions" (source: Partnerize)
- Mobile-first market: smartphone penetration exceeds 90%, 5G widespread (source: Charlesworth Group)
- Livestream shopping emerging as sales channel (source: Charlesworth Group)

## 7. Key Market Conclusions

1. **The market is large and growing.** Japan's e-commerce market (USD 207--287 billion) and affiliate marketing market (JPY 438 billion) provide a massive revenue base.

2. **The bilingual niche is unserved.** 4.13 million foreign residents and 42.7 million annual tourists have no dedicated English-language product comparison resource for Japan.

3. **Regulatory tailwinds favor quality.** Japan's 2023 anti-stealth-marketing rules penalize low-quality affiliate spam, creating competitive advantage for transparent, editorial-quality sites like Chartedly.

4. **Conservative Year 1 revenue of JPY 1.8M is achievable** with 50K monthly visitors and standard affiliate conversion rates.

5. **Year 3 revenue potential of JPY 28.8M** positions Chartedly as a meaningful revenue stream within the LAIRIA portfolio.
