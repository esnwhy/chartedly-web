## 1. Competitor Profiles

### 1.1 mybest (my-best.com)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Positioning** | "Japan's leading product recommendation service" -- expert-tested, data-driven product rankings |
| **Headquarters** | Tokyo, Japan |
| **Monthly traffic** | 30+ million monthly unique users in Japan (source: mybest PR Newswire, Nov 2023) |
| **Key features** | In-house product testing lab; expert/creator recommendations; category-specific scoring rubrics; international expansion to 8 countries including US |
| **Languages** | Japanese (primary), English, and 6+ other languages via regional sites |
| **Pricing model** | Free to users; revenue from affiliate links (Amazon JP, Rakuten) and sponsored placements |
| **Strengths** | Massive traffic; credibility from physical product testing; deep category coverage (est. 100,000+ products); strong SEO in Japanese-language search |
| **Weaknesses** | English-language site (us.my-best.com) has significantly less content and weaker SEO; UX is utilitarian list-based layout -- no discovery or browsing experience; overwhelming volume makes casual browsing difficult |
| **Opportunity gap** | Chartedly can differentiate with a Netflix-style visual browsing experience, stronger English-language focus for Japan's 4+ million foreign residents, and curated "collections" rather than exhaustive lists |

### 1.2 Kakaku.com (kakaku.com)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Positioning** | Japan's dominant price comparison and user review platform |
| **Monthly traffic** | 67.96 million monthly visits as of September 2025 (source: SimilarWeb) |
| **Key features** | Real-time price tracking across retailers; user reviews and ratings; spec comparison tables; price history charts; subsidiary Tabelog (restaurant reviews) |
| **Languages** | Japanese only |
| **Pricing model** | Free to users; revenue from retailer CPC advertising, affiliate commissions, and premium listing fees |
| **Strengths** | Enormous traffic; deep trust among Japanese consumers; comprehensive electronics/appliance coverage; price-tracking utility creates return visits |
| **Weaknesses** | Entirely Japanese-language -- zero accessibility for non-Japanese speakers; dated UI/UX (information-dense, text-heavy); primarily electronics-focused; no editorial curation or lifestyle positioning |
| **Opportunity gap** | Chartedly targets the entire English-speaking audience that Kakaku ignores; lifestyle-forward design vs. spec-sheet approach; mobile-first Netflix UX vs. desktop-era layout |

### 1.3 @cosme (cosme.net)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Positioning** | Japan's authoritative beauty and cosmetics review platform (since 1999) |
| **Monthly traffic** | 17.6 million monthly active users; 10.3+ million registered members (source: @cosme official) |
| **Key features** | User reviews and ratings; Best Cosmetics Awards (biannual); brand/product database; @cosme STORE physical retail; @cosme TOKYO flagship |
| **Languages** | Japanese (primary); limited English content |
| **Pricing model** | Free to users; revenue from brand advertising, sponsored content, and physical retail (@cosme STORE) |
| **Strengths** | Unmatched authority in Japanese beauty; massive review corpus; biannual awards generate media coverage; omnichannel (online + physical stores) |
| **Weaknesses** | Beauty-only vertical -- no coverage of other product categories; Japanese-language-centric; dated web interface; mobile app experience inconsistent |
| **Opportunity gap** | Chartedly covers beauty AND all other lifestyle categories in one place; bilingual from day one; modern browsing-oriented UX vs. review-centric UX |

### 1.4 Wirecutter (nytimes.com/wirecutter)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Positioning** | "The best products, chosen by our team" -- New York Times-owned expert product recommendation site |
| **Monthly traffic** | Estimated 15--20 million monthly visits (US-focused) |
| **Key features** | In-depth expert testing and long-form reviews; "Our Pick / Budget Pick / Upgrade Pick" framework; NYT editorial standards; clean, readable design |
| **Languages** | English only |
| **Pricing model** | Free (with NYT paywall for some content); affiliate revenue (primarily Amazon US) |
| **Strengths** | Gold-standard editorial quality and trust; simple pick hierarchy reduces decision fatigue; NYT brand authority; excellent SEO |
| **Weaknesses** | US-centric product availability -- does not cover Japan-specific products; no Japanese language; subscription paywall friction; no visual discovery/browsing experience |
| **Opportunity gap** | Chartedly applies Wirecutter-level curation to Japan-available products; no paywall; Netflix-style browsing adds discovery element Wirecutter lacks; bilingual serves Japan's international community |

### 1.5 Rotten Tomatoes (rottentomatoes.com) -- UX Reference

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Positioning** | Aggregated critic and audience scores for movies/TV -- used here as a UX benchmark, not a direct competitor |
| **Key features** | Tomatometer (critic aggregate) + Audience Score dual-score system; Certified Fresh badge; genre browsing; "What to Watch" curated lists |
| **Languages** | English |
| **Relevance to Chartedly** | The dual-score system (expert + community) is a strong UX pattern for product trust signals; "Certified Fresh" translates directly to a "Chartedly Certified" badge concept; genre-based browsing maps to category-based product discovery |
| **Strengths** | Instantly recognisable scoring iconography; aggregation reduces individual review bias; clean at-a-glance decision-making |
| **Weaknesses** | Scores can be gamed; limited context behind the number; ad-heavy experience |
| **Opportunity gap** | Chartedly can adopt the dual-score (editorial + community sentiment) and badge system while providing deeper product context (specs, pricing, where-to-buy) |

## 3. Strategic Positioning Summary

Chartedly occupies a **clear whitespace** in the Japan product recommendation market:

1. **No major player serves English-speaking residents of Japan** with curated product recommendations for locally-available goods. mybest has a US site for US products; Wirecutter covers US products. Neither helps a foreigner in Tokyo decide which rice cooker to buy on Amazon Japan.

2. **No competitor uses a Netflix-style visual discovery UX.** All existing sites use traditional list/grid layouts. Research shows 77% of Netflix users struggle with choice paralysis in traditional interfaces (source: UX Collective, Netflix Discovery UX Case Study) -- a visual, curated browsing experience reduces decision fatigue.

3. **The bilingual gap is enormous.** Japan has 4.13 million foreign residents (Japan Times, Oct 2025) and welcomed 42.7 million tourists in 2025 (Nippon.com). These users currently have zero dedicated English-language product comparison resources for Japan.

4. **Curation over volume.** mybest has 100,000+ products; Kakaku has millions of price listings. Chartedly will win by being *selective* -- 500 carefully curated products in Year 1, each with clear "Best For" designations, modeled on Wirecutter's pick hierarchy.
