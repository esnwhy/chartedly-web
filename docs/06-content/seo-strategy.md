## 4. Technical SEO Checklist

### Meta Tags

- [ ] Every page has a unique `<title>` tag (50-60 characters)
- [ ] Every page has a unique `<meta name="description">` (120-155 characters)
- [ ] `<meta name="robots" content="index, follow">` on all content pages
- [ ] `<meta name="robots" content="noindex, follow">` on search results, tag archives, paginated archives beyond page 1
- [ ] Open Graph tags (`og:title`, `og:description`, `og:image`, `og:url`, `og:type`) on all pages
- [ ] Twitter Card tags (`twitter:card`, `twitter:title`, `twitter:description`, `twitter:image`) on all pages
- [ ] `<meta name="author" content="Chartedly">` on all editorial pages

### Structured Data (JSON-LD)

- [ ] **Product Schema** on every comparison article (each reviewed product)
- [ ] **ItemList Schema** on ranking/comparison pages (ordered list of products)
- [ ] **FAQPage Schema** on articles with FAQ sections
- [ ] **HowTo Schema** on guide articles with step-by-step instructions
- [ ] **Article Schema** on every content page (datePublished, dateModified, author)
- [ ] **Organization Schema** on homepage (logo, sameAs for social profiles)
- [ ] **BreadcrumbList Schema** on all pages (Phase 2, when category hierarchy exists)
- [ ] Validate all structured data with Google Rich Results Test before publishing

### Hreflang (When Japanese Content Launches)

```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en" href="https://chartedly.com/best-japanese-sunscreen" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ja" href="https://chartedly.com/ja/best-japanese-sunscreen" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://chartedly.com/best-japanese-sunscreen" />
```

- [ ] Every hreflang page has self-referencing tags
- [ ] Use ISO 639-1 language codes (en, ja)
- [ ] Canonical URLs stay within their respective language version
- [ ] Validate hreflang with Ahrefs or Screaming Frog (75% of implementations have errors)

### Sitemap

- [ ] Auto-generate `sitemap.xml` with Astro's `@astrojs/sitemap` integration
- [ ] Include all indexed pages with `<lastmod>` dates
- [ ] Exclude utility pages (privacy, 404, search results, admin)
- [ ] Submit to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools on day one
- [ ] Include image sitemaps for product photos and comparison charts

### Canonical URLs

- [ ] Self-referencing `<link rel="canonical">` on every page
- [ ] Use absolute URLs: `https://chartedly.com/best-japanese-sunscreen`
- [ ] Force HTTPS via Cloudflare; redirect HTTP to HTTPS
- [ ] Use non-www (`chartedly.com`) and redirect www
- [ ] No trailing slashes; enforce via redirect

### Core Web Vitals Targets

| Metric | Good Threshold | Chartedly Target |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) | < 2.5s | < 1.8s |
| INP (Interaction to Next Paint) | < 200ms | < 150ms |
| CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) | < 0.1 | < 0.05 |

### Page Speed Optimization

- [ ] Serve images in WebP/AVIF with explicit width/height attributes
- [ ] Lazy-load images below the fold
- [ ] Preload hero images and critical fonts
- [ ] Minimize JavaScript (Astro's island architecture helps)
- [ ] Use Cloudflare CDN edge caching
- [ ] Compress all assets with Brotli
- [ ] Font display: swap for web fonts

### Indexation Controls

**Index (default):** All comparison articles, category pages, guide pages, about page, homepage.

**Noindex:** Tag archives, search results pages, preview/draft pages, paginated archives beyond page 1, thin pages with fewer than 300 words.

### Robots.txt

```

Allow: /

Disallow: /api/
Disallow: /admin/
Disallow: /_astro/
Disallow: /search?
Disallow: /preview/

Sitemap: https://chartedly.com/sitemap-index.xml
```

## 5. Link Building Strategy for a New Site

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

**Goal:** Establish basic backlink profile and indexation.

| Tactic | Expected Links | Effort | Authority Impact |
|--------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| Google Search Console submission | 0 (indexation) | Low | Foundation |
| Reddit engagement (r/japanlife, r/japantravel, r/AsianBeauty) | 3-5 referral links | Medium | Low DA but high relevance |
| Japan expat Facebook groups | 2-3 referral links | Medium | Social signals |
| Pinterest product boards | 5-10 pins with links | Medium | Referral traffic + indexation |
| HARO / Connectively responses | 1-2 mentions | High effort | Medium-High DA |

**Rules for Reddit/community engagement:**
- Be genuinely helpful first. Answer questions, then mention Chartedly only when directly relevant.
- Never spam links. Build credibility in the community over weeks before linking.
- Target specific threads: "What sunscreen should I buy in Japan?" -- answer thoroughly, then link the full comparison as a resource.

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 3-6)

| Tactic | Expected Links | Effort | Authority Impact |
|--------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| Guest posts on Japan expat blogs (GaijinPot, Savvy Tokyo, Tokyo Cheapo) | 2-3 contextual backlinks | High | Medium-High DA |
| Create shareable visual assets (infographics, comparison charts) | 3-5 natural backlinks | Medium | Varies |
| Broken link building on Japan travel resources | 2-4 replaced links | High | Medium DA |
| HARO / journalist outreach (J-beauty trending topic) | 2-3 mentions | High | High DA |
| Internal cross-linking between all articles | 30+ internal links | Low | Crawl efficiency |

### Phase 3: Authority (Months 6-12)

| Tactic | Expected Links | Effort | Authority Impact |
|--------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| Original research ("We surveyed 500 foreigners in Japan about...") | 5-10 citation links | Very High | High DA |
| Japanese brand collaboration (provide English translation value) | 1-2 .co.jp backlinks | Medium | Very High DA |
| Listicle inclusion ("Best Japan resources" roundups) | 3-5 links | Medium | Varies |
| Podcast guest appearances (Japan-focused podcasts) | 2-3 links | Medium | Medium DA |
| Content syndication partnerships | 2-3 canonical backlinks | Medium | Medium-High DA |

### Internal Linking Rules

- Every comparison article links to 2-3 related comparisons within body text.
- Every comparison article links back to its pillar/category page.
- "Related Comparisons" section at the bottom of every article with 3 linked articles.
- Footer lists all major articles (at MVP scale).
- New articles link to existing articles; existing articles updated to link to new articles.
- Anchor text is descriptive and keyword-relevant: "our sunscreen comparison" not "click here."

## 7. AI Search Optimization (2026 Considerations)

AI search engines (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity) increasingly drive zero-click answers. Content strategies must adapt:

- **Structure content for citation.** Clear H2/H3 headings, concise answer paragraphs, structured data. AI systems cite well-organized content.
- **Pillar-cluster architecture increases AI citation probability by 2.7x** compared to standalone pages.
- **Include definitive statements.** "The best Japanese sunscreen for most people is the Biore UV Aqua Rich" is more citable than hedging language.
- **Maintain freshness.** AI systems favor recently updated content. Update comparison articles every 90 days.
- **Build E-E-A-T signals.** Author bios, methodology pages, transparent affiliate disclosures. AI systems evaluate trustworthiness.
- **Do not rely exclusively on Google.** Diversify traffic: email newsletter, Pinterest, Reddit, direct bookmarks.
