## Primary Goals
1. **Build a curated shopping list before arriving in Japan** — he wants to know exactly which products are worth buying (skincare, electronics, kitchen knives, whiskey) so he can maximize limited shopping time during the trip
2. **Understand what makes Japanese products special** — he wants context, not just rankings. Why is this knife better? What makes this skincare ingredient unique? He values editorial depth alongside ratings
3. **Find tax-free shopping guidance and price expectations** — he wants to know what things cost in Japan versus back home, where to buy tax-free, and whether to buy in Tokyo or Osaka

## Pain Points
1. **Overwhelming "what to buy in Japan" articles are generic and ad-driven:** Every travel blog has a "30 Best Things to Buy in Japan" listicle, but they're all the same surface-level content (Kit-Kats, Uniqlo, etc.) with affiliate links. He can't find genuinely expert-curated, category-deep rankings. *Synthesized from analysis of tourist shopping guides (Atlys, LIVE JAPAN, Japan Experience) and travel blog content patterns.*
2. **No pre-trip product research tool exists:** TripAdvisor covers restaurants and hotels but not product shopping. Reddit has scattered recommendations but no structure. He has to manually compile a shopping list from dozens of sources. *Synthesized from Reddit travel planning threads (JapanFlo analysis of 1,600+ comments) and TripAdvisor forum discussions.*
3. **Fear of tourist traps and overpaying:** He's heard that some popular tourist shopping areas (Akihabara for electronics, Shinsaibashi for cosmetics) mark up prices for tourists. He wants reliable price benchmarks and honest "skip this" recommendations. *Synthesized from Japan travel regret analyses (JapanFlo, GoWithGuide) and tourist shopping guides (Tokyo Cheapo, kakaku.com coverage).*

## Scenario Narrative
It's Saturday afternoon, six weeks before Marco and Sara's Japan trip. Marco has a Google Sheet with tabs for "Itinerary," "Restaurants," and "Shopping." The shopping tab is sparse — he's written "Japanese knives," "skincare for Sara," "electronics??" and "whiskey." He opens Google and searches "best Japanese skincare for tourists 2026."

The first five results are listicles with titles like "20 Must-Have Products!" — each list has different products, sponsored placements, and no clear methodology. He clicks through three of them and notices they all mention SK-II and Hada Labo but disagree on everything else. He opens Reddit r/JapanTravel and searches "skincare" — the results are fragmented across dozens of posts spanning 4 years.

Two hours later, he has 8 browser tabs open and a vague sense that he should buy "something from Matsumoto Kiyoshi" but no concrete list. He closes his laptop, frustrated, and tells Sara, "I'll figure it out when we get there."

**If Chartedly existed:** Marco opens Chartedly and browses "Japan Shopping Guide > Skincare." He sees a curated visual grid with clear categories: "Moisturizers," "Sunscreens," "Sheet Masks," "For Beginners." Each product has a ranking, price range, and "Where to Buy" tag (drugstore, department store, airport). He taps "Tourist Favorites" filter and gets a refined list. In 15 minutes, he has a definitive shopping list with store addresses saved to Google Maps.
