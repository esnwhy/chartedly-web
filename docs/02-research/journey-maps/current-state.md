# Current-State Journey Map

> How each persona currently discovers, evaluates, and purchases products in Japan — before Chartedly exists.

---

## Emma Chen (28, American Expat)

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Needs a product (e.g., humidifier, skincare, cleaning supply) or sees a recommendation in a Reddit thread / LINE group.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Googles in English; browses r/japanlife; asks expat friends on LINE |
| Touchpoints | Google Search, Reddit, LINE groups, Instagram |
| Thoughts | "Is there an English resource for this in Japan?" |
| Emotions | Hopeful but already anticipating friction |
| **Friction** | **English-language product information for Japan-specific products is extremely scarce. Google results return global products, not Japan-market items.** |

### Stage 2: Consideration
**Activity:** Researching and comparing specific products.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Opens mybest (EN), @cosme, kakaku.com, Amazon JP. Screenshots Japanese text for Google Lens translation. Cross-references Reddit threads. |
| Touchpoints | mybest, @cosme, kakaku.com, Amazon JP, Google Lens, Reddit |
| Thoughts | "The English mybest article covers 15 products but feels outdated. @cosme has great data but I can't read it." |
| Emotions | Frustrated, mentally fatigued |
| **Friction** | **Must use 4-6 platforms simultaneously. Translation workflow is slow and error-prone. No single source provides English rankings for Japan-market products.** |

### Stage 3: Decision / Purchase
**Activity:** Committing to a product.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Defaults to the product with the most Reddit upvotes or the Amazon JP best-seller. Orders on Amazon JP or visits nearest drugstore. |
| Touchpoints | Amazon JP, Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Don Quijote |
| Thoughts | "I'm 70% confident this is the right choice. Good enough." |
| Emotions | Resigned acceptance, slight buyer anxiety |
| **Friction** | **Decision is based on incomplete information. Low confidence leads to post-purchase doubt.** |

### Stage 4: Post-Purchase
**Activity:** Evaluating the product after use.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | If satisfied, posts recommendation in Reddit/LINE group. If dissatisfied, absorbs the loss — returning products in Japan is culturally discouraged and logistically difficult. |
| Touchpoints | Reddit, LINE, personal social circle |
| Thoughts | "I wish I'd known about [alternative product] before buying this." |
| Emotions | Satisfied or regretful, with no easy feedback loop |
| **Friction** | **No structured way to share learnings back to a platform. Knowledge stays siloed in ephemeral Reddit comments and LINE messages.** |

## Yuki Tanaka (22, Japanese Student)

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Sees a trending product on TikTok, Instagram, or through friend recommendations on LINE.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Saves TikTok/Instagram post. Screenshots product name. Sends to friends on LINE asking "これ知ってる？" (Do you know this?) |
| Touchpoints | TikTok, Instagram, LINE |
| Thoughts | "This looks amazing but is it really that good?" |
| Emotions | Excited, curious, slightly skeptical of influencer content |
| **Friction** | **Discovery is passive and algorithm-driven. She can't control what she discovers or systematically browse categories.** |

### Stage 2: Consideration
**Activity:** Verifying the product's quality and comparing alternatives.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Opens @cosme to check rankings and reviews. Reads reviews filtered by age and skin type. Watches YouTube review videos. Compares prices on Amazon JP vs. Rakuten. |
| Touchpoints | @cosme, YouTube, Amazon JP, Rakuten, LIPS app |
| Thoughts | "It has good reviews on @cosme but I need to see it on someone with my skin tone." |
| Emotions | Engaged but overwhelmed by volume of information |
| **Friction** | **@cosme is text-heavy and not visually engaging. No cross-category comparison tool exists. Visual product comparison requires jumping between Instagram, YouTube, and @cosme.** |

### Stage 3: Decision / Purchase
**Activity:** Committing or deferring.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Often defers purchase — adds to mental wishlist, waits for payday, or waits to see if a friend buys it first. Eventually buys at drugstore or Amazon JP. |
| Touchpoints | Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Amazon JP, Rakuten |
| Thoughts | "Is this really the best one or should I wait?" |
| Emotions | Decision paralysis, budget anxiety |
| **Friction** | **Decision paralysis from too many options and no clear "best pick" signal. Price sensitivity extends deliberation period. 40%+ of discovered products are never purchased.** |

### Stage 4: Post-Purchase
**Activity:** Sharing experience.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Posts photo on Instagram stories. Writes @cosme review if strongly positive or negative. Recommends (or warns) friends on LINE. |
| Touchpoints | Instagram, @cosme, LINE |
| Thoughts | "My friends need to know about this — or to avoid it." |
| Emotions | Satisfaction (if good purchase) or frustration shared socially |
| **Friction** | **Feedback is fragmented across platforms. @cosme reviews are valuable but feel like a chore to write on mobile.** |

## Marco Rossi (35, Italian Tourist)

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Starts planning Japan trip. Knows Japan is famous for quality products but doesn't know specifics.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Googles "best things to buy in Japan 2026." Reads 3-5 listicle articles. Browses r/JapanTravel. Creates Google Sheet for shopping list. |
| Touchpoints | Google Search, travel blogs, Reddit r/JapanTravel |
| Thoughts | "Japan has amazing products — I want to bring back the best stuff." |
| Emotions | Excited, ambitious about planning |
| **Friction** | **Listicles are generic, repetitive, and affiliate-driven. All recommend the same 10 surface-level items. No depth by category.** |

### Stage 2: Consideration
**Activity:** Trying to build a specific shopping list by category.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Searches Reddit for category-specific recommendations ("best Japanese knife for home cook"). Attempts to use kakaku.com via Google Translate. Watches YouTube "Japan haul" videos. Asks friends who've visited Japan. |
| Touchpoints | Reddit, kakaku.com (translated), YouTube, personal network |
| Thoughts | "Every source gives different recommendations. Who do I trust?" |
| Emotions | Overwhelmed, losing confidence in his research |
| **Friction** | **No structured, tourist-oriented product comparison platform exists. kakaku.com is unusable without Japanese. Reddit recommendations are unstructured and often outdated. No way to verify which products are genuinely best vs. tourist-marketed.** |

### Stage 3: Decision / Purchase (In Japan)
**Activity:** Shopping during the trip with limited time.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Arrives at Don Quijote or Matsumoto Kiyoshi and feels overwhelmed by selection. Defaults to products he recognizes from pre-trip research. Buys some items impulsively. Misses tax-free threshold at some stores. |
| Touchpoints | Don Quijote, Matsumoto Kiyoshi, department stores, airport shops |
| Thoughts | "Is this the one I read about? There are 50 sunscreens here — which one??" |
| Emotions | Overwhelmed, time-pressured, some buyer's remorse |
| **Friction** | **Gap between pre-trip research and in-store reality. Can't quickly verify products in-store. No mobile-optimized reference tool. Tax-free rules are confusing.** |

### Stage 4: Post-Trip
**Activity:** Evaluating purchases, sharing with friends.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Shows purchases to friends. Some products are great; others were impulse buys he regrets. Friends ask for recommendations — he struggles to remember specific product names and stores. |
| Touchpoints | WhatsApp, Instagram, in-person social sharing |
| Thoughts | "I wish I'd bought more of [good product] and skipped [bad product]." |
| Emotions | Mixed satisfaction, some regret, desire to help friends planning their trips |
| **Friction** | **No platform to save and share a "Japan shopping list" with friends. Product knowledge gained during the trip dissipates quickly.** |

## Aiko Yamamoto (40, Japanese Working Mother)

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** A household product breaks, runs out, or a seasonal need arises (e.g., new school year supplies for kids).

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Asks mama-tomo LINE group first. Googles if no one responds. Sees Amazon JP recommendations in her browsing history. |
| Touchpoints | LINE mama-tomo groups, Google JP, Amazon JP |
| Thoughts | "Does anyone have a recommendation? I don't have time to research." |
| Emotions | Mildly stressed, looking for a shortcut |
| **Friction** | **Relies on social network which may not have relevant experience. Awareness of new products is limited by her time-constrained media consumption.** |

### Stage 2: Consideration
**Activity:** Evaluating options under severe time constraints.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Opens Amazon JP — sorts by best-seller or highest-rated. May open mybest article but reads only the top 3 recommendations before running out of time. Checks kakaku.com specs if it's an appliance purchase. |
| Touchpoints | Amazon JP, mybest, kakaku.com |
| Thoughts | "This mybest article is 8,000 words. I don't have time for this." |
| Emotions | Frustrated, fatigued, decision-averse |
| **Friction** | **Existing platforms are designed for thorough researchers, not time-poor users. No "just tell me the answer" mode exists. Information density exceeds her available attention span.** |

### Stage 3: Decision / Purchase
**Activity:** Making a purchase under time pressure.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | Defaults to familiar brands or Amazon best-seller. Orders on Amazon JP with Prime delivery. For urgent needs, buys at nearest drugstore/home center during lunch break. |
| Touchpoints | Amazon JP, neighborhood stores |
| Thoughts | "This is probably fine. I can't spend more time on this." |
| Emotions | Resignation, low confidence, mild guilt about not researching more |
| **Friction** | **Satisficing instead of optimizing. Systematically overpays or gets sub-optimal products because she can't afford the time cost of proper research.** |

### Stage 4: Post-Purchase
**Activity:** Living with the product.

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Actions | If the product is good, mentions it in mama-tomo LINE group when asked. If bad, absorbs the cost — no time or energy to return it or write a review. |
| Touchpoints | LINE, direct word-of-mouth |
| Thoughts | "If only I'd had 5 more minutes to research, I might have found something better." |
| Emotions | Acceptance, occasional regret |
| **Friction** | **Never writes online reviews — time cost is too high. Her product knowledge stays in closed social circles, unavailable to the broader community.** |
